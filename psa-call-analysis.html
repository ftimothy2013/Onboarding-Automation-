🧠 Key Insights

What 566 calls tell us about where the friction lives — and where automation has the highest leverage.

🔁

Same 4 Questions. Every Call.

Ticketing workflow, integrations, payment processor, and data migration come up in 84–90% of calls. Sales already knows the answers. This is a handoff gap, not a discovery gap.

⏱️

411 Hours of SA Time

In 5 months, across 3 SAs, that's over 17 days of non-stop client calls. A significant portion is spent re-collecting info, not configuring. Time-to-value starts the moment this flips.

📊

Billing + Ticketing = 95% of Every Session

These two configuration areas are touched in virtually every call. They are the onboarding. If billing and ticketing could be pre-scaffolded from sales data, sessions would start at Step 3, not Step 1.

🐯

TigerPaw Is Everywhere

Tiger Paw / Tigerpaw appears in 42+ calls — a disproportionate share of the client base. A dedicated Tigerpaw-to-Rev.io migration playbook and pre-configuration template would eliminate the most common migration re-work cycle.

📋

Action Items Are Repetitive

SA follow-up tasks cluster around the same 8–10 patterns: login setup, CSV templates, GL mapping, payment processor follow-up, user account creation. These should be automated pre-kickoff, not manually tracked post-call.

🚀

Highest Automation ROI

Pre-configure: customer record, user accounts + roles, billing profile, and QuickBooks integration before kickoff. These four items appear in the top 6 configuration categories and can be seeded from sales call data alone.

🎯 The Automation Target: 4 Questions × 566 Clients

If the sales team captures just 4 data points at close — (1) prior PSA, (2) accounting software, (3) payment processor, (4) number of users + roles — then billing setup, user account creation, integration configuration, and migration scoping can begin before the SA ever schedules a call. That's the difference between Day 1 starting blank and Day 1 starting at 60% configured.
